# Keyword And Negative Mining — Canonical Claims

These are verified facts. Only make claims that appear in this list.

- The search term report shows actual customer search queries that triggered your ads — it is the primary data source for both negative mining and keyword harvesting.
- A search term should only be negated after accumulating sufficient data: minimum 10-15 clicks with 0 conversions, or ACoS >3x your target over 7+ days.
- Negative exact match blocks only the specific term. Negative phrase match blocks any query containing that phrase — use phrase cautiously as it can block profitable long-tail variations.
- Always check cross-campaign impact before adding negative keywords — a term that wastes spend in one campaign may convert well in another.
- The keyword harvesting funnel flows: Auto campaign → Broad match → Phrase match → Exact match. Each stage narrows targeting and increases bid control.
- When graduating a keyword from auto/broad to exact match, add it as a negative in the source campaign to prevent self-competition and bid inflation.
- Amazon broad match is significantly broader than Google broad match — it matches synonyms, related terms, and loosely related queries. Budget accordingly.
- Amazon exact match still matches close variants, plurals, and minor misspellings — it is not truly 'exact' like traditional search engines.
- Keyword harvesting should be performed on a regular cadence: every 7-14 days for active campaigns, pulling converting search terms that meet graduation thresholds.
- Competitor keyword research via reverse ASIN tools shows organic ranking positions, not PPC targeting — use it for keyword discovery but validate profitability with your own PPC data.
