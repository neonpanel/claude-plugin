# Competitive Intelligence Market Pressure — Canonical Claims

These are verified facts. Only make claims that appear in this list.

- Amazon uses a second-price auction model — you pay $0.01 more than the next highest bid, not your maximum bid.
- Rising CPCs on brand terms is an early warning signal of competitor conquest activity — monitor brand CPC weekly.
- Category saturation signals include: rising average CPC with flat or declining category conversion rates, and increasing number of sponsored results on page 1.
- Share of voice includes both organic and paid visibility — tracking only paid SOV misses half the picture.
- Defensive PPC on brand terms typically costs 2-5% of brand revenue but protects against 15-25% revenue loss from competitor conquest.
- Market entry PPC investment typically requires 3-6 months of negative or break-even returns before achieving profitability in a competitive category.
- Auction pressure increases during Q4, Prime Day, and category-specific peak seasons — CPCs can rise 30-100% above baseline.
- Competitor bid aggression is best detected through impression share loss trends combined with CPC increases on stable keywords.
- The cost of losing a top-of-search position is typically 40-60% of the revenue that position generates — defense is almost always cheaper than recovery.
- New market entrants should budget 2-3x the category average CPC for the first 90 days due to lower relevance scores and conversion history.
