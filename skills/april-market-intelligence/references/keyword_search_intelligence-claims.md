# Keyword Search Intelligence — Canonical Claims

These are verified facts. Only make claims that appear in this list.

- Search Query Performance (SQP) shows your impression share, click share, and purchase share relative to the TOTAL market for each query — not just your PPC performance.
- Low click share with high impression share in SQP indicates a listing problem (main image, title, price) rather than a PPC problem.
- Search Catalog Performance (SCP) reveals which of your ASINs appear for which queries, enabling detection of internal cannibalization.
- The search funnel has four stages: impressions → clicks → add-to-cart → purchases. Each stage has different optimization levers.
- Brand keywords typically have 3-5x higher conversion rates than generic keywords and 5-10x higher than competitor keywords.
- Brand defense campaigns should target a TACoS below 3-5% — if brand CPC rises significantly, it signals competitor conquest activity.
- Share of voice (SOV) should be weighted by keyword search volume — a 50% SOV on a 100-search keyword matters less than 10% SOV on a 100,000-search keyword.
- Keyword clustering by intent (informational vs transactional vs navigational) enables more precise bid strategies — transactional keywords warrant higher bids.
- SQP data requires a minimum of 4 weeks to be statistically meaningful — shorter periods produce unreliable impression and purchase share numbers.
- Click share ÷ impression share = relative CTR. A ratio below 1.0 means your listing underperforms the category average CTR.
