# Competitive Intelligence & Market Pressure Playbooks

## Detect Competitor Bid Aggression

1. Establish baseline metrics for brand terms: average CPC, impression share, click share (4-week average).
2. Monitor weekly for deviation signals:
   - Brand term CPC increase > 20% above baseline
   - Impression share drop > 10% on brand terms
   - New competitor ASINs appearing in brand search results
3. If aggression detected:
   - Increase brand term bids by 20-30% to reclaim position
   - Check if competitor is running Sponsored Brands on your brand name
   - Assess competitor's product (price, reviews, rating) to gauge threat level
4. Implement counter-strategy:
   - Short-term: increase brand defense budget
   - Medium-term: run conquest campaigns on competitor's brand terms
   - Long-term: improve listing conversion to make your position more defensible
5. Track weekly until competitor activity normalizes.

## Assess Category Saturation

1. Pull 6-month trend data for the category:
   - Average CPC trend
   - Number of sponsored results on page 1
   - Category conversion rate trend
   - Number of new product launches
2. Calculate saturation indicators:
   - CPC growth rate vs revenue growth rate (if CPC grows faster, saturation increasing)
   - Sponsored result density (>8 sponsored placements on page 1 = high saturation)
   - Conversion rate decline (falling CVR with rising CPC = classic saturation signal)
3. Assess impact on your business:
   - Calculate your break-even CPC at current conversion rate
   - Project when category CPC will exceed your break-even
4. Develop response strategy:
   - Differentiate: improve listing, add unique value props
   - Niche down: target long-tail keywords with less competition
   - Diversify: expand to adjacent categories
   - Accept: increase prices to support higher CPCs

## Evaluate Market Entry Opportunity

1. Define the target category and top 10 keywords.
2. Research current competitive landscape:
   - Number of established competitors (100+ reviews)
   - Average CPC for top keywords
   - Average review count of page 1 products
   - Price range and margin potential
3. Model entry investment:
   - PPC budget: estimate 3-6 months at category average CPC × target click volume
   - Inventory: 90-day supply at target velocity
   - Listing: professional photography, A+ content, video
4. Calculate time-to-profitability:
   - Month 1-3: expect negative contribution margin (investment phase)
   - Month 4-6: target break-even on PPC
   - Month 7+: target positive contribution margin
5. Score the opportunity: market size × margin potential × competitive feasibility.
6. Go/no-go decision based on required investment vs expected return.

## Build Defensive PPC Model

1. Identify critical keywords: top 20 keywords by revenue contribution.
2. Calculate revenue at risk for each keyword if position is lost.
3. Estimate defense cost: current CPC × required clicks to maintain position.
4. Calculate defense ROI: revenue protected ÷ defense cost.
5. Prioritize defense by ROI (highest ROI keywords get defended first).
6. Set defense budget: typically 2-5% of total revenue from defended keywords.
7. Implement automated defense rules:
   - If impression share drops below 70% on critical keyword → increase bid by 15%
   - If brand term CPC increases > 25% → alert for manual review
8. Review defense performance monthly and adjust keyword priority list.
